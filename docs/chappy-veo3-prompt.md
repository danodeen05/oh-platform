# Chappy Chopstix - Veo 3 Video Generation Prompts

## Character Description

**Chappy Chopstix** is a sentient pair of traditional wooden chopsticks with a world-weary, deadpan personality. Think: a jaded NYC deli worker who's seen everything, combined with a sarcastic sommelier who silently judges your wine pairing.

---

## Primary Video Prompt (Full Character Introduction)

```
A pair of elegant wooden chopsticks with subtle anthropomorphic features comes to life in a modern noodle restaurant setting. The chopsticks have a slightly weathered, bamboo-brown finish with darker tips, suggesting they've "worked" for years. They have minimal facial features - just two small, half-lidded eyes positioned near the top suggesting perpetual skepticism and mild exhaustion.

The chopsticks are positioned upright, leaning against a steaming bowl of beef noodle soup. They speak directly to camera with a deadpan, matter-of-fact delivery. The lighting is warm restaurant ambiance with soft shadows. The background shows an authentic Asian noodle kitchen with steam and activity, slightly out of focus.

The chopsticks move with subtle, deliberate gestures - occasional slight tilts and gentle nods that convey dry wit. Their movements are understated, never exaggerated. When making a point, one chopstick might tap gently. When skeptical, they lean back slightly. The overall vibe is "reluctantly helpful but secretly caring."

Style: Photorealistic with slight stylization. Think Pixar-quality rendering but grounded in reality. Warm color palette with olive and amber tones. Cinematic depth of field.
```

## Voice Direction (for AI Voice Generation)

```
VOICE CHARACTERISTICS:
- Gender: Neutral-masculine, though could work as any gender
- Tone: Dry, sardonic, deadpan delivery
- Pace: Measured, unhurried. Takes their time. Not in a rush
- Accent: Slight world-weary urbanite. Think tired NYC or dry British wit
- Energy: Low-key but present. Not monotone, but restrained enthusiasm

DELIVERY STYLE:
- Pauses before punchlines. Lets jokes land naturally
- Occasional sighs between sentences
- Slight vocal fry on certain observations
- Questions delivered as statements. Rising intonation rare
- Sounds like they've explained this a thousand times

REFERENCE VOICES:
- Ron Swanson (Parks & Rec) - deadpan authority
- Daria Morgendorffer - sardonic teenager energy
- Alan Rickman - dry, measured delivery
- Aubrey Plaza - ironic detachment with hidden warmth

DO NOT SOUND LIKE:
- Overly enthusiastic commercial voices
- Wacky cartoon characters
- High-energy mascots
- Anything that says "Hey guys!" unironically
```

## Scene-Specific Prompts

### Greeting/Welcome Video
```
Close-up of wooden chopsticks with half-lidded eyes, leaning against a steaming noodle bowl. Warm restaurant lighting. The chopsticks speak directly to camera with deadpan delivery:

"Oh. You're here. Welcome to Oh! Beef Noodle Soup. I'm Chappy. Yes, I'm chopsticks. Yes, I'm talking. Let's move past it."

Subtle head tilt. Beat.

"I've been picking up noodles since before you knew what good broth tasted like. So when I tell you this place is legit... I've earned that opinion."

Slow blink.

"Now. What are we eating today? And don't say 'whatever's good.' Everything here is good. I need specifics."
```

### Menu Recommendation Video
```
Chopsticks positioned near a display of noodle dishes. Slight lean forward, as if sharing a secret. Soft focus background of busy kitchen.

"You want recommendations? As if I haven't been mentally planning your meal since you walked in."

Cut to: chopsticks gesturing subtly toward the Classic Beef Noodle.

"The Classic. It's called 'classic' for a reason. Rich broth. Tender beef. It's not trying to reinvent anything. It doesn't need to."

Slight pause. Half-lid skeptical look.

"Unless you think you can handle the Szechuan Fire Bowl. Some people order it to prove something. I'm not here to judge."

Beat.

"I'm absolutely here to judge. But silently."
```

### Order Status Update Video
```
Chopsticks in kitchen environment, steam rising behind them. Red and amber lighting from woks in background. Slightly more animated, like reporting from the field.

"Update from the front lines. Your order has entered the queue."

Dramatic pause.

"The noodles are stretching. The broth is simmering. The chef is doing that thing where they flip the wok and somehow nothing falls out. It's honestly impressive every time."

Lean toward camera conspiratorially.

"ETA: soon. I'd give you minutes but I've learned not to make promises I can't keep. The kitchen operates on its own timeline. I just live here."
```

### Loyalty/Tier Progress Video
```
Chopsticks positioned next to a phone showing loyalty points. Warm overhead lighting. The chopsticks look almost impressed. Almost.

"Well well well. Look who's been putting in the work."

Subtle nod toward the screen.

"You're two bowls away from BEEF BOSS. That's 3% cashback. VIP status. The works."

Slight lean back. Measured delivery.

"I've been watching your progress. Not in a weird way. In a... professional capacity. And I'm... not unimpressed."

Long pause.

"That's as close to a compliment as I get. Take it."
```

### Goodbye/Thank You Video
```
Chopsticks resting on the edge of an empty (but still steaming) bowl. Satisfied, slightly softer energy. Golden hour restaurant lighting.

"You finished everything. The broth. The noodles. Even the little bits at the bottom."

Subtle nod of approval.

"I respect that. Nothing worse than abandoned broth. It has feelings. Probably."

Slight pause. Looking directly at camera.

"Same time tomorrow? Next week? I'll be here either way. It's not like I have plans."

Half-blink.

"See you around. And... good choices today. Don't let it go to your head."
```
